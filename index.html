Operating principles

A coordinated approach, not a centralised one.

Country ownership and sovereignty
Governments retain full control over systems, data, and how solutions are evolved over time.
Driven by public service outcomes
Success is measured by real-world impact on citizens — access, inclusion, service quality.
Built for interoperability
Open interfaces and non-proprietary standards enable integrated, end-to-end services without lock-in.
Security by design
Strong security practices ensure that systems, data and integrations are protected and trusted.
Sustainable by design
Affordable to run, maintainable by a competitive ecosystem, with active upstream contribution.
Complementarity over competition
DPGs are positioned to work together as components of integrated services — not as rival platforms.

Reference · 03

Country case studies

Real-world deployments showing how governments have used DPG building blocks to deliver integrated public services. These patterns are repeatable.

Sri Lanka · 2022 — present

Integrated digital public services for 22 million citizens

Sri Lanka has connected DHIS2, MOSIP, OpenCRVS and OpenSPP into a coherent national stack — anchored by an in-country Digital Government Experience Centre that supports cross-ministerial use cases for civil registration, health and social protection.

Outcomes & impact

Hospital-to-registry integration

DHIS2-to-OpenCRVS integration via OpenFn enables automatic birth notifications from maternity units, reducing manual processing.

Digital identity from birth

Integration with MOSIP enables every registered child to receive a foundational digital identity at the point of birth.

Social protection eligibility

Civil registration records feed OpenSPP to support automated eligibility assessment for mothers and newborns.

Reduced processing time

Average time to receive a birth certificate reduced from 4–6 weeks to under 5 days through digitisation and integration.

Philippines · 2021 — present

Identity-linked social protection at national scale

The Philippines has paired MOSIP-based foundational identity with OpenSPP for social protection and OpenCRVS for civil registration — enabling identity-anchored eligibility, payments and beneficiary management across one of the world's largest social protection programmes.

Outcomes & impact

Identity-anchored eligibility

PhilSys identity binding into OpenSPP enables deduplicated, verifiable beneficiary records across programmes and ministries.

Faster, more accurate payments

Identity-linked payment rails reduce leakage and ghost beneficiaries, with cycle times shortened and reconciliation automated.

Cross-ministerial coordination

Shared infrastructure connects DSWD social protection programmes with PSA civil registration through standard interfaces.

Progressive adoption

Started with identity, layered on social protection, then civil registration — demonstrating staged, low-risk adoption of the stack.

Partner ecosystem

System integrators with demonstrated capability across multiple Digital Public Goods. Partners deliver integrated, cross-DPG solutions and contribute back to the platforms and assets they implement.

Common standards · all listed partners

Open delivery standards

Every partner listed below operates against a shared set of standards designed to protect your ownership of the systems you procure, sustain the underlying Digital Public Goods, and maintain a competitive ecosystem of implementers.

Multi-DPG capability
Active implementation or support of at least two DPGs in the collaborative, with a pathway toward three or more.
Open architectures
Implementations use open standards and open interfaces — no proprietary lock-in layers.
Transferable delivery
Systems can be maintained, extended or transferred to other partners by your government over time.
Reusable assets
Contribution of integration components, blueprints or playbooks to the shared collaborative asset base.
Upstream contribution
Adherence to each DPG's sustainability mechanism — DHIS2 Shared Services Fee or equivalent contribution models.
Security & data ethics
Documented security practices, privacy-by-design, and ethical data handling across all engagements.
Country leadership
Engagements led with your government as primary actor — partner roles are clear, time-bound and visible.
Continuous activity
Listing maintained through ongoing implementations or contributions; inactive members lose visibility.
